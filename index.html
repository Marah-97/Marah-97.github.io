00 ‚Äî INTRODUCTION

The Dataset

This analysis draws on two public datasets published by the San Francisco Police Department on the SF Open Data Portal: historical incident reports from 2003 to May 2018, and incident reports from 2018 to the present. After merging and standardizing the two datasets, the combined record spans 22 years of reported crime across all police districts in the city.

From this combined dataset, we selected seven Personal Focus Crimes that together offer a broad picture of urban crime patterns ‚Äî ranging from violent offences to property crime and public order violations:

Assault Vehicle Theft Drug Offences Burglary Robbery Suspicious Sex Offence

These crimes were chosen to capture a variety of crime types ‚Äî violent, property-based, and enforcement-driven ‚Äî so that contrasting trends across categories can reveal something meaningful about how social and policy changes shape the city's crime landscape.

01

Drop in assault during COVID

There is a noticeable drop in recorded assault offences around 2020. COVID restrictions limited nightlife and public gatherings, reducing face-to-face interactions and opportunities for interpersonal violence.

02

Surge in burglary & vehicle theft during COVID

The pandemic produced the opposite effect on property crime. Empty buildings, reduced street activity, and economic stress created more opportunities for burglary and vehicle theft.

03

Drug offences: decline then surge 2021‚Äì22

Drug offenses declined steadily until the pandemic, driven by reduced enforcement and a shift toward treatment over arrest. By 2024, they rose above pre-pandemic levels as SF increased enforcement in response to the visible fentanyl crisis.

04

General decline in robberies

Robbery shows a long-term downward trend. Cashless payments, increased surveillance, and smartphone tracking have reduced both the opportunity and payoff of robbery over two decades.

Reading the heatmap

Each cell shows the ratio of how common a crime is in a given district compared to the city-wide average. A value of 1.0 means exactly average; 2.0 means twice as common; 0.5 means half as common. This normalization allows fair comparison across districts of different sizes.

The Tenderloin stands out

The Tenderloin is the most striking district in the heatmap, with a drug offences ratio of 3.28 ‚Äî meaning drug crime is more than three times as common there compared to the city average. At the same time, vehicle theft is heavily under-represented (0.25), which makes sense given that the Tenderloin is a dense, pedestrian-heavy neighborhood with fewer privately parked vehicles.

The Tenderloin has long been the center of San Francisco's open-air drug market. The city declared a state of emergency there in late 2021 in response to overdose deaths and street disorder. This data confirms that pattern with striking clarity.

Other notable patterns

Out of SF stands out for vehicle theft (2.04) and sex offences (1.86), likely reflecting incidents near highways or city boundaries where vehicles are more vulnerable. Richmond and Taraval show elevated burglary ratios (1.42 and 1.24) ‚Äî quieter residential western neighborhoods with more homes and shops but far less street-level drug activity.

Central and Northern districts show relatively balanced profiles close to 1.0 across most categories, reflecting their mixed-use tourist and commercial character ‚Äî crime there broadly mirrors the city-wide distribution.

To investigate how incident times are recorded in the dataset, we selected assault incidents between 13:00‚Äì14:00 over a three-month window (January‚ÄìMarch 2015).

The most striking pattern is a large cluster at minute 0 ‚Äî far larger than any other minute ‚Äî indicating that officers frequently round to the nearest full hour when filing reports. A smaller cluster at minute 30 is also visible, consistent with rounding to the half hour.

This reveals an important limitation of the data: recorded times should not be treated as exact measurements. For broad hourly analysis this rounding is not a major problem, but any fine-grained sub-hour analysis would be unreliable.
